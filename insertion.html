Insertion Sort

Insertion sort is a simple sorting algorithm that works similar to the way you sort playing cards in your hands. The array is virtually split into a sorted and an unsorted part. Values from the unsorted part are picked and placed at the correct position in the sorted part.

How it works

Consider an array arr[] = {12, 11, 13, 5, 6}

    First Pass:

    • Initially, the first two elements of the array are compared in insertion sort. arr[] = {12, 11, 13, 5, 6}
    • Here, 12 is greater than 11 hence they are not in the ascending order and 12 is not at its correct position. Thus, swap 11 and 12.
    • So, for now 11 is stored in a sorted sub-array. arr[] = {11, 12, 13, 5, 6}

    Second Pass:

    • Now, move to the next two elements and compare them. arr[] = {11, 12, 13, 5, 6}
    • Here, 13 is greater than 12, thus both elements seems to be in ascending order, hence, no swapping will occur. 12 also stored in a sorted sub-array along with 11.

    Third Pass:

    • Now, two elements are present in the sorted sub-array which are 11 and 12
    • Moving forward to the next two elements which are 13 and 5 arr[] = {11, 12, 13, 5, 6}
    • Both 5 and 13 are not present at their correct place so swap them arr[] = {11, 12, 5, 13, 6}
    • After swapping, elements 12 and 5 are not sorted, thus swap again arr[] = {11, 5, 12, 13, 6}
    • Here, again 11 and 5 are not sorted, hence swap again arr[] = {5, 11, 12, 13, 6}
    • Here, it is at its correct position

    Fourth Pass:

    • Now, the elements which are present in the sorted sub-array are 5, 11 and 12
    • Moving to the next two elements 13 and 6 arr[] = {5, 11, 12, 13, 6}
    • Clearly, they are not sorted, thus perform swap between both arr[] = {5, 11, 12, 6, 13}
    • Now, 6 is smaller than 12, hence, swap again arr[] = {5, 11, 6, 12, 13}
    • Here, also swapping makes 11 and 6 unsorted hence, swap again arr[] = {5, 6, 11, 12, 13}
    • Finally, the array is completely sorted.
